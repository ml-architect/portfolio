## About the Project

The project was developed for the [RANEPA LIA](https://lia.ranepa.ru/) educational platform — it is an interactive educational case study where students go through the full Data Science cycle: from exploratory analysis of real data to building an ML model and formulating business recommendations.

The domain is retail in the sterile area of Sheremetyevo International Airport (SVO). The application works with real data from May-June 2022: retail outlet revenue, passenger traffic by flight, schedules, weather conditions, and destination airport coordinates. The central question: what drives store revenue in the departure zone, and can it be predicted?

## Architecture

The application is a Streamlit app organized as a sequential analytical pipeline of six stages. Each stage is a collapsible section that guides the student from data familiarization to final conclusions.

In the first stage, the user explores the source datasets: revenue tables in 30-minute intervals, passenger traffic logs with flight numbers, terminals, and airlines, flight schedules with aircraft types, airport and airline reference tables, and a weather data archive in METAR format.

The second stage is exploratory analysis. The application builds a series of interactive charts: passenger traffic distribution by airline and terminal, flight rankings by load, top retail outlets by revenue, and revenue dynamics by hour and day. Each chart is accompanied by an analytical commentary that directs the student's attention to key patterns — for example, that Aeroflot provides several times more passenger traffic, and revenue peaks fall on Fridays.

In the third stage, hypotheses are formulated and tested. Data from different sources is merged into a single table: weather parameters, passenger traffic information, temporal features (hour, day of week, weekend flag), and geographic data are added to the revenue records. The distance from Sheremetyevo to each destination airport is calculated using geodesic coordinates sourced from the open Flightradar24 database. Four hypotheses are tested: the impact of weather, time of day, weekends, and flight distance on revenue.

The fourth stage is model training. CatBoost gradient boosting is used in regression mode with time-series cross-validation that correctly respects the chronological order of data and prevents future data leakage. Three variants are trained for comparison: a model on enriched data (weather, coordinates, time), a model on raw data without enrichment, and a naive baseline that predicts revenue from the previous 30-minute interval. Results are compared using RMSE, MAE, and R2 metrics.

The fifth stage is interactive verification of conclusions. The student independently determines which hypotheses were confirmed and which were not. Three out of four were confirmed: revenue depends on weather (bad weather delays passengers in the departure zone), time of day, and day of the week. The hypothesis about the impact of flight distance was not confirmed.

The final stage is a seven-question test on the case material to verify comprehension.

## Features

- Full Data Science cycle: from EDA to ML model and business recommendations
- CatBoost regression model for forecasting 30-minute retail outlet revenue
- Data enrichment: merging 7 sources (revenue, passenger traffic, schedules, weather, coordinates)
- Geospatial analytics — calculating distances from SVO to destination airports using geodesic coordinates
- 10+ interactive Plotly visualizations with analytical commentaries
- Interactive knowledge assessment quiz

## Tech Stack

**Application:** Python, Streamlit 1.15

**ML:** CatBoost 1.1, scikit-learn 1.2 (TimeSeriesSplit)

**Data and Visualization:** pandas 1.5, numpy 1.23, plotly 5.11, geopy 2.3

**Deployment:** Docker Compose
