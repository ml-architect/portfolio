## About the Project

The History Department at MIPT is digitizing archival collections from pre-revolutionary Russia — governor reports and statutory charters from the 18th–19th centuries. The scans contain damage, faded ink, and non-standard typefaces; manual transcription of a single document takes up to an entire workday. Standard OCR solutions (Tesseract, EasyOCR) cannot handle pre-revolutionary orthography, handwritten script, and atypical layouts.

The goal was to build an ML pipeline for automatic text recognition on such scans.

## Architecture

Three-stage pipeline:

**1. Text block detection.** YOLOv8 locates text regions, separating them from decorative elements, seals, and artifacts. For training, 2,000+ images were annotated in Roboflow, marking headings, body text, and marginalia.

**2. Segmentation and preprocessing.** Text regions are cropped and normalized: skew correction, adaptive binarization, and morphological operations via OpenCV. This restores readability even on heavily damaged fragments with minimal contrast.

**3. Text recognition.** A seq2seq model based on Hugging Face Transformers, fine-tuned on a corpus of pre-revolutionary texts. It accounts for old orthography (yat, fita, yer) and handles both printed and handwritten text. The output is transcribed text mapped to coordinates on the scan.

The pipeline runs through JupyterLab — archivists visually control each stage.

## MLOps

ClearML — experiment tracking: metric comparison, model versioning, hyperparameter logging. Roboflow — annotation and dataset version management with the ability to incrementally fine-tune when adding documents from new regions.

## Tech Stack

**ML:** PyTorch 2.2 (CUDA 11.8), Ultralytics (YOLOv8), Hugging Face Transformers 4.37, Datasets, scikit-learn

**CV:** OpenCV, Roboflow, Supervision

**MLOps:** ClearML, JupyterLab 4.1

## Key Achievements

- **mAP50 93%** on text block detection — confident recognition even on damaged scans
- **2,000+ scans** from archives of 29+ regions (1804–1882)
- **~10x speedup** compared to manual transcription
- **Unique domain** — pre-revolutionary orthography and handwritten text, inaccessible to standard OCR
