## About the Project

A project for text recognition on historical documents from the 18th–19th centuries: governor reports and statutory charters of pre-revolutionary Russia. Working with handwritten and printed texts from archives of 29+ regions.

## Objective

Build an OCR pipeline that can detect text blocks on scans of antique documents (often damaged, with uneven handwriting) and recognize text — both handwritten and printed.

## Pipeline

**Detection** → **Segmentation** → **Recognition**

- **YOLOv8** — text block detection on scans
- **Roboflow** — professional data annotation
- **Hugging Face Transformers** — seq2seq models for OCR
- **ClearML** — experiment tracking and versioning

## Tech Stack

**ML:** PyTorch 2.2 (CUDA 11.8), Ultralytics (YOLOv8), Hugging Face Transformers 4.37, Datasets, scikit-learn

**CV:** OpenCV, Roboflow, Supervision

**MLOps:** ClearML, JupyterLab 4.1

## Key Achievements

- **Unique domain** — OCR of pre-revolutionary handwritten documents, a non-trivial task for CV/NLP
- **Object Detection + seq2seq** — combination of YOLOv8 for detection and Transformers for recognition
- **MLOps practices** — ClearML for tracking, Roboflow for annotation, data versioning
- **Real archive** — 29+ regions, governor reports from 1804–1882
