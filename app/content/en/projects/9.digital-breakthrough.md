## About the Project

"Digital Breakthrough" is the largest all-Russian digitalization competition where teams solve real-world problems for specific regions of the country within 48 hours. As part of this hackathon series, I participated in five projects, each requiring a completely different approach — from satellite imagery analysis and natural language processing to building recommendation systems. All solutions were developed for the public sector and aimed at automating processes that were previously performed manually.

## Projects

### Detection of Illegal Buildings via Satellite Imagery

**Region:** Udmurt Republic

The task was to automate the detection of unauthorized structures across the region using satellite imagery data. Manually inspecting thousands of square kilometers is impossible, so the solution was built on computer vision. High-resolution satellite images were sliced into patches and processed by a semantic segmentation model based on ResNet-50, which outlined building contours on each patch. Detected objects were cross-referenced with Russia's Federal Cadastral Registry (Rosreestr) data via API to determine whether a building was officially registered. In parallel, YOLOv5-based object detection was applied, converting pixel coordinates of detected buildings into geographic coordinates and automatically checking their cadastral status.

### Classification of Citizen Appeals

**Region:** Kursk Oblast

The regional administration receives hundreds of written appeals from citizens daily — complaints, suggestions, requests — and each one needs to be routed to the appropriate department. Manual routing slowed down processing and led to errors. The solution was built on fine-tuning BERT-family language models for Russian: base BERT, lightweight ruBERT-tiny, and Sentence-BERT for working with text embeddings. The models were trained on a labeled corpus of real appeals and automatically determined the topic category of each text, enabling instant routing to the corresponding department.

### Classification of Municipal Infrastructure Condition

**Region:** Republic of Bashkortostan

Municipal services in the region document infrastructure condition through photography, but manual sorting of thousands of images by problem categories is inefficient. The task was to automatically determine the type and severity of an issue from a photo. The solution was built on transfer learning of deep convolutional networks: Xception with fine-tuning of the top layers and EfficientNet-B7 with a full augmentation pipeline and dynamic learning rate scheduling. The models classified images into three problem categories, with special attention to handling imbalanced classes — class weighting and thorough data cleaning of mislabeled samples were applied.

### Museum Exhibit Search

**Region:** Lipetsk Oblast

Regional museums store over five thousand exhibits, and related items — duplicates, series, objects from the same era — are often found across different collections. The task was to find similar exhibits across the entire catalog given a query (image or description). The solution was based on building vector representations for each exhibit — both visual (based on features extracted by a convolutional network) and textual (from metadata and descriptions). Nearest neighbor search in the embedding space enabled rapid discovery of semantically similar items even when no exact match existed in the catalog.

### Library Recommendation System

**Region:** Tyumen Oblast

The regional library network had accumulated an extensive borrowing history — tens of thousands of transactions across a catalog of nearly one hundred thousand books. The task was to suggest personalized recommendations to readers based on their reading history. The solution used a hybrid approach: collaborative filtering via LightFM accounted for behavioral patterns of similar readers, while the content-based component built on Transformers constructed vector representations of books from their descriptions and metadata. For fast nearest neighbor search among hundreds of thousands of embeddings, a FAISS index was used. Final recommendations were generated iteratively — through 12 refinement rounds, the model progressively improved the quality of selections.

## Key Achievements

- **5 real-world cases** for the public sector — each project solved a specific problem for a specific region under 48-hour hackathon conditions
- **Full spectrum of ML tasks** — computer vision, natural language processing, recommendation systems, and information retrieval within a single competition series
- **Satellite imagery + cadastral data** — non-trivial integration of computer vision with government geoinformation systems
- **Working with unique data** — from photos of municipal infrastructure violations to museum exhibit catalogs and library collections
- **State-of-the-art models** — BERT for Russian-language texts, YOLO and EfficientNet for images, LightFM and FAISS for recommendations
