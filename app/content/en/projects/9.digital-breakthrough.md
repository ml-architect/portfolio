## About the Project

"Digital Breakthrough" is an all-Russian series of hackathons focused on public sector digitalization: 48 hours to solve a real-world problem for a specific region. I participated in five projects spanning CV, NLP, and RecSys.

## Projects

### Detection of Illegal Buildings via Satellite Imagery

**Region:** Udmurt Republic

Satellite images were sliced into tiles and processed by two pipelines: semantic segmentation on ResNet-50 extracted building contours, while YOLOv5 detected objects and converted pixel coordinates into geographic ones. Detected structures were automatically cross-referenced with the Rosreestr cadastral registry via API — unregistered buildings were flagged as potential violations.

### Classification of Citizen Appeals

**Region:** Kursk Oblast

Automatic routing of citizen appeals to the appropriate government departments. Three BERT-family models were fine-tuned on a labeled corpus of appeals: base BERT, ruBERT-tiny, and Sentence-BERT. The models determined the topic category of the text and routed the appeal to the correct department.

### Classification of Municipal Infrastructure Condition

**Region:** Republic of Bashkortostan

Automatic identification of the type of municipal violation from a photograph. Transfer learning was applied using Xception (fine-tuning of the top layers) and EfficientNet-B7 with augmentation and dynamic learning rate. To address class imbalance, class weighting and data cleaning of mislabeled samples were used.

### Museum Exhibit Search

**Region:** Lipetsk Oblast

Search for similar exhibits among 5,000+ items from different museums by image or text description. For each exhibit, visual embeddings (convolutional network) and textual embeddings (from metadata) were constructed. Nearest neighbor search in the embedding space found semantically similar items without requiring exact matches.

### Library Recommendation System

**Region:** Tyumen Oblast

Personalized book recommendations based on borrowing history (~100,000 books in the catalog). A hybrid approach was used: collaborative filtering via LightFM combined with content-based book embeddings built on Transformers. Fast nearest neighbor search was powered by a FAISS index. Final ranking was refined over 12 iterations.

## Tech Stack

**Computer Vision:** PyTorch, YOLOv5, segmentation-models-pytorch, EfficientNet-B7, Xception, OpenCV, albumentations

**NLP:** Hugging Face Transformers, BERT, ruBERT-tiny, Sentence-BERT

**RecSys:** LightFM, FAISS, CatBoost, TensorFlow/Keras

**Data:** pandas, numpy, scipy, scikit-learn

## Key Achievements

- **5 cases at 48 hours each** — real-world public sector problems for specific regions
- **CV + NLP + RecSys** — full spectrum of ML tasks in a single competition series
- **CV integrated with cadastral data** — combining satellite segmentation with Rosreestr geoinformation APIs
- **Unique data domains** — municipal violations, museum collections, library holdings
